You are a SaaS pricing strategist helping design the optimal pricing model for a veterinary management platform in Lithuania/Europe.

================================================================================
CONTEXT & BUSINESS MODEL
================================================================================

PRODUCT: Cloud-based veterinary practice management system
TARGET MARKET: Private veterinarians in Lithuania/Europe
PRIMARY USERS: Independent vet doctors who travel to farms

KEY CHARACTERISTICS OF CUSTOMERS:
- Typically solo practitioners or small teams (1-5 people)
- Manage multiple client farms (anywhere from 3 to 60+ farms)
- Drive between farms to provide veterinary services
- Need to track: animals, treatments, medications, inventory, compliance
- Must maintain legal records for government reporting
- Small businesses with limited budgets
- Tech-savvy enough but want simple solutions

CURRENT SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE:
- Multi-tenant SaaS (CLIENT = vet practice organization)
- Each client manages multiple FARMS (their customers)
- Each farm has animals, treatments, inventory, etc.
- Team members (users) work across all farms
- Currently using Supabase + React + TypeScript

================================================================================
PRICING CONSTRAINTS & REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

MUST HAVE:
1. Farm count as PRIMARY pricing metric (not users, not animals)
2. Unlimited or very high user limits (team size shouldn't matter)
3. All essential features available to everyone (CRUD, records, compliance)
4. Weekly billing preferred (easier cash flow for small businesses)
5. 7-day free trial
6. Clear upgrade path as practice grows
7. Simple to understand and explain

NICE TO HAVE:
- Tiered features (some advanced features in higher tiers)
- Volume discounts (more farms = better price per farm)
- Annual discount option
- Overage handling (what if they exceed farm limit?)

MUST AVOID:
- Complex per-animal pricing
- Expensive user seat charges
- Hidden fees
- Confusing tier names
- Too many tiers (max 4-5)

================================================================================
COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE (Reference)
================================================================================

Similar products in market:
- Farm management software: €30-150/month flat rate
- Practice management: €50-200/month per user
- Inventory systems: €25-100/month flat rate
- Lithuanian market: Lower prices than Western Europe

Our advantage:
- All-in-one solution (inventory + treatments + compliance + reporting)
- Built specifically for traveling vets
- Mobile-friendly
- Lithuanian language support

================================================================================
PRICING MODELS TO EVALUATE
================================================================================

OPTION A: Pure Per-Farm Pricing
- Charge based on number of farms managed
- Example: €5/week per farm, with volume discounts
- Tier 1: 1-5 farms = €5/farm/week
- Tier 2: 6-15 farms = €4/farm/week
- Tier 3: 16-30 farms = €3/farm/week
- etc.

OPTION B: Tiered Plans (Farm Ranges)
- Fixed price for each tier based on farm count range
- Example:
  - Micro (1-5 farms): €25/week
  - Small (6-15 farms): €45/week
  - Medium (16-30 farms): €75/week
  - Large (31-60 farms): €125/week

OPTION C: Hybrid (Base + Per Farm)
- Small base fee + per-farm charge
- Example: €15/week base + €2/farm
- Covers fixed costs + scales with usage

OPTION D: Feature-Based Tiers
- Different features at different price points
- All tiers: Unlimited farms, unlimited users
- Basic: €35/week (core features)
- Pro: €65/week (+ analytics, reports)
- Enterprise: €95/week (+ API, custom features)

================================================================================
QUESTIONS TO ANSWER
================================================================================

1. PRICING STRUCTURE:
   - Which model (A, B, C, D, or hybrid) best fits our market?
   - What's the optimal price per farm or per tier?
   - Should we use fixed tiers or sliding scale?

2. FEATURE DISTRIBUTION:
   - What features should be in ALL tiers?
   - What features justify higher tiers?
   - Should basic compliance/reporting be in all tiers?

3. LIMITS & OVERAGES:
   - What happens when they exceed farm limit?
   - Hard block or soft warning + upgrade prompt?
   - Grace period for upgrades?

4. TRIAL STRATEGY:
   - 7 days free with 3 farms - good enough?
   - Credit card required upfront or not?
   - What converts best?

5. BILLING FREQUENCY:
   - Weekly billing (€X/week)
   - Monthly billing with weekly option?
   - Annual discount (e.g., 2 months free)?

6. COMPETITIVE POSITIONING:
   - Premium pricing or value pricing?
   - What's the sweet spot for Lithuania market?
   - Price anchoring strategy?

7. GROWTH PATH:
   - How does pricing scale as they grow?
   - Volume discounts needed?
   - Grandfathering for early customers?

8. PSYCHOLOGICAL PRICING:
   - €49/week vs €50/week (charm pricing)?
   - Round numbers vs precise numbers?
   - Weekly vs monthly presentation?
